Living Labs

Five city Living Labs — real-world environments where innovative urban mobility solutions are co-created, tested, and scaled.

Brașov, Romania

Reducing Congestion with Low-Emission Zones & Micro-Mobility. Brașov's Living Lab focuses on improving air quality and accessibility through pedestrian-first policies and last-mile transport innovation.

Osmangazi, Türkiye

Connecting Osmangazi's City Square and Historical Center with Multimodal Transportation. The lab explores integrated networks linking public transport, cycling, and walking routes to support sustainable tourism and local mobility.

Rethymno, Greece

Fostering Safer Micro-Mobility Strategies for Tourists and Community. This Living Lab advances shared mobility and safer infrastructure for both residents and seasonal visitors in a historic coastal city.

Bochum, Germany

Optimizing Logistics to Reduce Car Dependency. Bochum's lab focuses on freight consolidation and smart delivery strategies to improve efficiency while minimizing environmental impact in urban cores.

Cologne, Germany

Inclusive, Sustainable Transport Solutions to Enhance Urban Accessibility. Cologne aims to integrate accessibility-focused planning with inclusive community engagement to make mobility equitable for all.
